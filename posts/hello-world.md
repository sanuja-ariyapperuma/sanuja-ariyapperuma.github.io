Why I'm starting to write publicly after seven years of building in silence.

I've spent the last seven years shipping production systems — C#, .NET, cloud infrastructure, React — but almost nothing publicly. No blog, no Twitter threads, no conference talks. Just code going into private repos and deployed systems.

That changes now.

## What changed

The arrival of AI-assisted engineering isn't just a productivity story. It's changing how I think about software design itself. When Claude Code can hold the whole architecture in context and GitHub Copilot can predict the next three methods, the bottleneck shifts. It's no longer "can I write this code?" It's "can I specify what I actually want precisely enough that the machine gets it right?"

That question is worth thinking about in public.

## What I'll write about

- Structured AI workflows: BMAD V6, Matt Pocock's skill sets, and what actually works vs. what's demo-ware
- .NET 10 in production: Minimal APIs, security patterns, the parts that don't make it into tutorials
- The gap between "AI-generated" and "AI-assisted" — and why it matters for quality

## The format

Short. Opinionated. Grounded in what I've actually shipped — not what I read in a newsletter.
